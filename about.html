Our Story

Built Around Execution

Marula Fund Managers is a 100 percent black owned, impact-driven venture capital manager established to mobilise and deploy institutional capital into early-stage and first commercial technology ventures aligned with South Africa's development priorities.

The firm is structured as a R100 million innovation fund, targeting an initial R30 million seed commitment from TIA, with individual investments ranging between R1 million and R10 million deployed through equity, convertible equity, or debt instruments.

Marula Fund Managers is not positioned as a passive capital allocator. It operates as an intervention-based venture builder that integrates structured project preparation, institutional-grade governance, disciplined deal execution, and technology-enabled portfolio monitoring.

"Ambition is common. Structured execution is not. Marula Fund Managers is built around execution."

Our Approach

Investment Philosophy

Our philosophy is clear, defensible, and uncompromising. That is the spine of the fund.

1

Capital Follows Readiness

We do not fund ideas. We fund validated execution pathways. Every investment must survive technical scrutiny, market realism, financial stress testing, and governance review before capital is deployed.

2

Early Truth Protects Returns

We surface fatal flaws before scale. Revenue is stress tested. Costs are conservative. Risks are explicit. If a venture cannot survive downside scenarios, it does not enter the portfolio.

3

Structure Drives Performance

Risk is deliberately priced and allocated through disciplined instruments, milestone-based deployment, and aligned governance. Hidden risk creates volatility. Structured risk creates returns.

4

Execution Determines Value

Scaling is earned, not assumed. Operational capacity, capital structure, regulatory alignment, and delivery readiness are validated before expansion comes.

5

Impact and Returns Are Interdependent

Transformation, ESG alignment, and inclusive growth are not concessions. They are competitive advantages that drive sustainable value creation. Impact is not treated as marketing language—it is embedded in sourcing, evaluation, and performance.
